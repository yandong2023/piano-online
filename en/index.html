Beginner piano learning guide

Start with keyboard layout

Learn where C, D, E, F, G, A, and B sit on the virtual piano. Play slowly and focus on clean note changes.

Practice short melodies

Simple songs build timing, finger confidence, and memory before you move to longer pieces.

Use rhythm mode carefully

Play at a relaxed speed first. Accuracy matters more than speed when you are just starting.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a real piano?

No. You can start with your computer keyboard or touch screen. A MIDI keyboard is optional.

Is this online piano free?

Yes. The virtual piano and beginner practice tools are free to use in your browser.

Can beginners use it?

Yes. Choose an easy song and follow the next-key prompts to build confidence step by step.

Does it support MIDI keyboards?

Yes, supported browsers can detect connected MIDI keyboards and let you play directly.

What keywords is this page for?

This page is designed for people searching for virtual piano, online piano, piano simulator, and play piano online.

Can I use it on mobile?

The piano supports touch interaction, but a desktop or tablet screen gives a better playing experience.
